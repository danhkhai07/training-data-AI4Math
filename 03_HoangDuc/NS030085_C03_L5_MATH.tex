\section*{Problem}

\text{Let $\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_n$ be integer vectors in $\mathbf{R}^n$ (i.e., vectors whose components are all integers). Prove that if $| \det(\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_n) | = 1$, then the parallelepiped $\mathbf{O}\mathbf{v}_1 \ldots \mathbf{v}_n$ contains no integer points other than its vertices. Here $\det(\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_n)$ is the determinant of the matrix with columns $\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_n$. Is the converse true?}
