\section*{Problem}

\text{Suppose $\mathbf{M}$ is a square matrix satisfying the equation $3\mathbf{M}^3 + 2\mathbf{M}^2 + 5\mathbf{M} + \mathbf{I} = \mathbf{0}$, where $\mathbf{I}$ is the identity matrix of the same size as $\mathbf{M}$. Is $\mathbf{M}$ invertible? If so, find the inverse matrix $\mathbf{M}^{-1}$.}

\section*{Solution}

\text{Starting with the given equation:}
$$
3\mathbf{M}^3 + 2\mathbf{M}^2 + 5\mathbf{M} + \mathbf{I} = \mathbf{0}
$$

\text{Isolating the identity matrix $\mathbf{I}$:}
$$
\mathbf{I} = -3\mathbf{M}^3 - 2\mathbf{M}^2 - 5\mathbf{M}
$$

\text{Factoring $\mathbf{M}$ on the right side:}
$$
\mathbf{I} = \mathbf{M}(-3\mathbf{M}^2 - 2\mathbf{M} - 5\mathbf{I})
$$

\text{Since $\mathbf{M}$ commutes with $\mathbf{M}^2$ and $\mathbf{I}$, we also have:}
$$
\mathbf{I} = (-3\mathbf{M}^2 - 2\mathbf{M} - 5\mathbf{I})\mathbf{M}
$$

\text{Let $\mathbf{B} = -3\mathbf{M}^2 - 2\mathbf{M} - 5\mathbf{I}$. We have found a matrix $\mathbf{B}$ such that $\mathbf{M}\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{B}\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{I}$.}

\text{Therefore, $\mathbf{M}$ is **invertible**, and its inverse is:}
$$
\mathbf{M}^{-1} = -3\mathbf{M}^2 - 2\mathbf{M} - 5\mathbf{I}
$$
