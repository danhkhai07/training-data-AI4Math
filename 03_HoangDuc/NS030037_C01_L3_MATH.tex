\section*{Problem}

\text{Let $\mathbf{A}$ be a $3 \times 3$ matrix, and $\mathbf{y} \in \mathbf{R}^3$ such that $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{y}$ has no solution.} \\
\text{Is it possible that $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{z}$ has a unique solution for some $\mathbf{z} \in \mathbf{R}^3$? Explain.}
